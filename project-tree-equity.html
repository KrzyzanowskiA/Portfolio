Second Street Through Time

A web application showcasing the changing streetscape of Second Street within the Jackson Ward neighborhood, created with the Historic Jackson Ward Association.

  • RoleStudent Researcher
  • PartnersHJWA · Virginia Commonwealth University
  • TimelineSummer 2026 - Present
  • ToolsArcGIS Pro, HTML, MapLibre

The question

Second Street was the heart of the Jackson Ward neighborhood in Richmond, Virginia. The street became a commercial corridor having financial institutions, insurance companies, and personal offices. Due to I-95 construction going right through the neighborhood and desegregation, the street started to become more and more vacant.

This project aimed to showcase what Second Street used to be and help educate people on its important history.

What I did

I started this project by georeferencing and drawing out parcels on old Sanborn and city planning maps. Then, I joined over directory data to see where businesses, residences, and institutions were located on the street. We then selected POIs for each of the six eras to highlight some noteworthy locations located on Second Street.

We then started working on a web application to create an interactive visual for people to use. We loaded in the basemaps as a tile layer and the parcels and POIs as GeoJSONS.

What I found

From this app we were able to observe how the streetscape changed over different eras. Starting off as mainly residential, changing to majority commercial, and to today’s layout. The web application allows people to see this change and how I-95 splitting Second Street was detrimental to businesses.

What I learned

Though none of the GIS concepts used in this project were new to me, I learned lots of new things. Through working on the web application, I had my first exposure to coding. More importantly, this project taught me the importance of working with a community on things that are important to them.
